WORKING FOR A BETTER AUSTIN.

Join me! Let's do it together.

My Service

I have been serving the community as a Deputy Constable for more than 25 years, working in Travis and Williamson Counties. I have a Master Peace Officers and Civil proficiency license with many years of experience with the service of civil process, warrant service, juvenile law and apprehension and community policing. I believe in taking my many years of knowledge and working with you, the community, to bridge the gap between Law Enforcement and the public. The best way is to get out into our community and work together as a team.

My Roots

My family moved to the Austin area in 1970 when my father was relocated from Lexington, KY to the Austin/Round Rock area. I attended and graduated from Round Rock High School and then attended the Police Academy in 1988. I started my Law Enforcement career with the Round Rock Police Dept. and then full time with Austin ISD as a School Officer. Approximately 20 years ago, I became a Deputy Constable for more opportunity to further my law enforcement career. I have lived in Cedar Park for the last 15 years and married to Dr. Amy Papst, an Anesthesiologist and hospital administrator, and we have two daughters ages 22 and 18. Our daughters both attend Texas Universities, one at the University of Texas – Austin and one at Texas State University. In the past 50 years I have seen Cedar Park, Leander and Liberty Hill go through some tremendous changes with the population growth. We choose to live in Cedar Park that still has the small town feeling with shopping, beautiful lakes, parks, local restaurants. We love being part of the Cedar Park community and all the local activities that occur year around. I am looking forward to remaining in Cedar Park and participating in its future.

My Mission

My mission is to provide a professional and knowledgeable Constable’s office. We would provide training for our deputies to become proficient in the service of civil process, state laws and community contact and policing. We would strive to provide exceptional service through the use of new technology to bring efficiencies to the civil process. I’m always looking for the most efficient use of tax payer’s dollars with the fastest way of receiving, serving and returning all civil process. I would ensure that all officers have mental health training, so they can have the skills necessary to assess the situation quickly, de-escalate whenever possible and keep the officers, individuals involved and public involved safe. Our officers will be proactive while in your neighborhoods serving civil process. Always watching and willing to help with a loose dog, get a missing stop sign replaced or stop and talk to homeowners about their concerns (“Community Officer”). My mission is connecting with our citizens to make a safer community to live in.
